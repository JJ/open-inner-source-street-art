Hackathons == Jams

In this activity organized here in 2016, called "HackTheWall", organized by the open source lab at the university of Granada (of which I was the head) and a touring thing called CirCoLab, we made two street artists collaborate with hardware and software hackers, creating a couple of amazing graffiti that were interactive and took a day of work by a rather big and eclectic group. Because hackatons/jams break silos, barriers between disciplines, but also across skill levels; you see my daughter there, who was 17 then and is now a software engineer in a company called Clarivate, and she's learning from Nake, a well-established street artist and illustrator; the two technicians in CirCoLab are doing technician things.

Street artists tag and slap

tags are signatures, slaps are stickers, sometimes with the "hello my name is..." shape, but some others simply custom-made stickers. These are the street-art equivalent of: I respect you, I like what you're doing... But also a way of transmitting techniques, colors, shapes. But the point is that the "art" is created through this collective action, sometimes intentional (jams) sometimes asynchronous and spontaneous
